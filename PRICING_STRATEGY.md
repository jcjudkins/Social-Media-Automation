# Pricing & Business Model Strategy

## Understanding Your Options

You have **3 main paths** for monetizing this platform:

### Option 1: **Outright Sale to DEFNA**
Sell all IP (code, brand, domain) to DEFNA for a one-time payment.

### Option 2: **SaaS with Monthly Revenue**
Keep ownership, charge monthly fees (like Buffer, Hootsuite).

### Option 3: **Hybrid: License to DEFNA + Run SaaS**
Give DEFNA exclusive rights to Django community, you run it for everyone else.

---

## Option 3: Hybrid Model (Recommended)

### How It Works
1. **License to DEFNA**: Give them exclusive rights for Django community
2. **You Run SaaS**: Operate it for everyone else
3. **Revenue Share or License Fee**: They pay you ongoing or upfront

### Structure Examples

**Example A: Exclusive License + You Run Platform**
```
DEFNA pays you:
- Upfront: $20,000 for exclusive Django community rights
- Monthly: $500/mo for hosting their instance
- Annual: $5,000/year license renewal

You operate:
- Platform for everyone else
- DEFNA gets white-label version
- You handle all technical operations
```

**Example B: Revenue Share**
```
DEFNA pays you:
- Upfront: $10,000 to help with development
- Revenue share: 20% of revenue from Django community users
- You keep: 100% of revenue from non-Django users

Everyone wins:
- DEFNA gets platform for their community
- You build broader SaaS business
- Both benefit from growth
```

**Example C: Partner Program**
```
No upfront payment, but:
- DEFNA gets free Enterprise plan
- They promote to Django community
- You give them 30% commission on referrals
- You keep full ownership and all other revenue
```

### Pros
‚úÖ Get some upfront cash from DEFNA
‚úÖ Keep long-term revenue potential
‚úÖ DEFNA becomes customer + promoter
‚úÖ Lower risk than pure SaaS
‚úÖ Best of both worlds

### Cons
‚ùå More complex agreement
‚ùå Need good legal contract
‚ùå Ongoing relationship to manage

## Negotiation Strategy

### Before Meeting DEFNA

**Do Your Research:**
1. What's DEFNA's budget? (They're a non-profit, likely limited)
2. What do they currently pay for social media tools?
3. How many Django community groups would use this?
4. Who makes purchasing decisions?

**Set Your Walk-Away Number:**
- Minimum you'd accept for outright sale: $________
- Minimum monthly revenue to make SaaS worth it: $________

### During Negotiation

**Start High, Expect to Come Down:**
```
Initial Ask: $100,000 outright
Expected Counter: $30,000 - $40,000
Settlement: $50,000 - $60,000
```

**Or Propose Options:**
"I'm flexible on structure. Here are three options:"

1. **Option A**: $75,000 - you own everything
2. **Option B**: $30,000 + $1,000/mo for 3 years (I host)
3. **Option C**: $15,000 + revenue share on Django users

"Which appeals to you?"

**Let Them Choose:**
- Gives them agency
- You learn their budget/preference
- Creates collaborative feeling

### Key Questions to Ask Them

1. "What budget range are you thinking?"
2. "When would you want to launch this?"
3. "Would you want to host it or have me host?"
4. "How many Django community groups would use it?"
5. "Could you help promote it to Django community?"
6. "Is this a one-time purchase or ongoing relationship?"

### Red Flags to Watch For

üö© "We don't have budget but..." (Offer equity? Partnership?)
üö© "Build it first, then we'll decide" (No commitment = risky)
üö© Lowball offer <$20k for full ownership (Undervaluing you)

## Legal Considerations

### If Selling Outright

**You need:**
- Asset Purchase Agreement
- IP transfer documentation
- Non-compete clause (what can you build next?)
- Payment terms (upfront vs installments)
- Warranties about code ownership
- Transition/support period terms

### If Licensing/Partnership

**You need:**
- License Agreement
- Scope of exclusivity (just Django? geographic?)
- Revenue share terms (if applicable)
- Termination clauses
- Service Level Agreement (SLA)
- Renewal terms

### **Get a Lawyer**
For any deal >$10,000, spend $1,500-3,000 on a lawyer to:
- Review terms
- Protect your interests
- Ensure it's legally sound
- Worth 2-3% of deal value for protection
